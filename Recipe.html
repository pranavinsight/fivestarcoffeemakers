Arabica Coffee:

Ingredients:

    tablespoons of finely-ground coffee
    1 tablespoon of ground cardamom
    1/4 cup of water, sugar (to taste).

Recipe:

Pour water and sugar into a small pot or Turkish briki and bring to a boil over medium heat. Once your water boils, carefully add the ground coffee and cardamom. Return to a boil and when the mixture foams, briefly lift it off the heat, allowing the foam to subside. Repeat this at least twice to achieve the right foam texture. Your Arabic coffee is now ready to drink!

Robusta Coffee:

Ingredients: Fresh, high-quality Robusta beans.

Recipe:

Start with less coffee grounds than you normally would as Robusta will taste very strong compared to Arabica. Change the extraction time and grind size to adjust the flavor profile. You can also try cold brew or blend with other beans.

Liberica Coffee:

Ingredients:

    2-3 tablespoons of finely ground Liberica coffee
    2 cups of water,
    sugar (to taste)
    1 tablespoon of crushed/smashed cardamom
    3-4 whole cloves (optional)
    1 teaspoon of rose water (optional).

Recipe:

In the Philippines, it is traditionally made by “seeping” the grinds in boiling water mixed with brown sugar or muscovado.

Excelsa Coffee:

Ingredients:

Fresh, high-quality Excelsa beans.

Recipe:

Excelsa beans require a darker roast at higher temperatures to fully develop. Light roasts just can’t pull enough flavor from the dense seeds. You can also try making cold coffee with Excelsa beans by adding sugar to cold strong coffee, mixing thoroughly to dissolve all the sugar, straining, and pouring into a glass. You can add a food ice cube if you like.
Remember, the perfect cup of coffee is subjective and depends on personal preference. Feel free to experiment with these recipes to find your perfect brew. Enjoy your coffee brewing journey! ☕
